This visualization looks at the humidity in different cities. The only pattern we can discern here is that there are very humid places all across the globe. This is probably because although the tropical rainforests get a lot of rain, many coastal cities in more temperate regions such as Seattle, Washington and London, UK also receive a lot of rain throughout the year. This graph could be taken to also indicate where major cities across the world are. Since our data only looked at cities that were big enough to be measured, and since most large cities are coastal we see humid cities all over the globe. Another trend we see here is that there seem to be more data points from north of the Equator. This may be because most people live in the northern hemisphere and that not many people live very far south.

Humidity also varies widely with altitude. That may be yet another variable that may be hidden in the data. Since there are mountain ranges all across the globe, and chains such as the Rockies span entire continents they can block humid air from the coast and make it very humid on one side while dry on the other side.
